Heqet

January 2024 – Present (In Progress)

Video Game

Game Developer, Composer, Sound Designer, Audio Programmer

Heqet is a single player underwater adventure puzzle game originally created by Leo Norton and further developed by himself, Devin Kansakar, Eli Silverstein, and Tyler Chung during Spring of 2024. The game is inspired by various other games such as Subnautica (2014) and Abzû (2016).

The game tells the story of a glowing frog named Anura, who is spawned in by "The Hands," a powerful being of nature, to destroy “The Orb,” an object with unknown origins. The player must solve puzzles to get to different areas and clear off objects on top of coral platforms in order to reach "The Orb" at the end of the game.

Heqet is based on many real-life elements and events. It is meant to raise awareness about the human impact on oceans in various ways such as climate change and anthropogenic sounds. The game is planned to be released publicly in the near future and is aimed at marine conservation organizations.

Illumity

March 2025 – Present (In Progress)

Video Game

Game Developer, Programmer

Illumity is a single player rhythm game created by Tyler Chung as a solo project in game artificial intelligence. The game focuses on creating a unique experience for the player by incorporating music into player-AI interactions.

In this game, the player must time attacks to defeat enemies in each level. In addition, the player must press the note key that corresponds to the note the enemy is playing. The player does less damage to an enemy if the player attacks early or late (or no damage if the player presses the wrong key). To aid the player, especially if they are not musically trained, each note corresponds to a color. The color changes of each enemy's light sync with their note changes, and the player may attempt to associate each note with a light color to help play the correct sequence.

The player has a couple of abilities. They can transpose all the enemies' notes up or down at any time to not only change how the music sounds, but to also possibly create a sequence of notes that are easier to play. In addition, the player can speed up the tempo (above the base tempo of 60 BPM) to fill up their power bar. They can then slow the tempo (below the base tempo) to use up their power bar. This allows for easier timings with attacks. Once their power bar is depleted, the tempo rises back to to the base tempo.

Illumity was created using Unreal Engine 5 C++ and Blueprint. The note sequences are built around a Quartz clock. This games serves as an exploration of audio as a core game mechanic and the balance between music and AI gameplay.
